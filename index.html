About Me

Creative Problem Solver

Coming from an artistic family, I grew up surrounded by creativity and traditional craftsmanship. What began as simple curiosity, watching my family work with different materials and techniques, naturally grew into a deep appreciation for physical art forms, especially lacquer arts.

Over the years, I've developed a strong passion for lacquer work, drawn to its meticulous process, rich textures, and the way it preserves cultural stories through layers of color and material. There's something uniquely powerful about the patience and precision it requires, and I've always felt connected to the tradition it carries.

My dream is to become an art manager, someone who not only supports artists but also helps bring meaningful artistic experiences to a wider audience. When I'm not working on my own pieces, you can usually find me visiting galleries, studying different art movements, or exploring new ways to elevate and preserve traditional lacquer art practices.
